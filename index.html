Highlights

  • There are 1121 federally-managed .gov domains. That’s about 250 fewer than there was five years ago.
  • Of those domains, 936 (83.5%) are live. That’s about the same percentage as we saw in 2014 and 2015, and has gone up about 10% over the past 10 years.
  • Of those live domains, 864 (92.31%) are reachable without the www. prefix. That’s 20 fewer than in 2015, but about a 17% increase.
  • 892 (95.3%) domains support HTTPS, a 2.5x increase since the last analysis, and an incredible jump from the one in four we last saw. Even more surprisingly 860 (91.88%) enforce HTTPS, up from one in 10 last time.
  • 1118 (99.73%) support HSTS, with 497 (44.34%) .govs on the HSTS preload list.
  • 263 (28.1%) domains simply redirect to another domain.
  • Of those live, non-redirected domains, 203 (30.16%) use the Drupal CMS, up from 10% in 2015. Usage of WordPress doubled in percentage going from 29 to 39 (4.17%) sites since 2015. Joomla usage went from 8 to 5 (0.53%) domains.
  • 389 (57.8%) unique domains use Google Analytics. Up from about one in four in 2014 and just 86 ten years ago.
  • It’s not always possible to detect the hosting provider, but at least 111 (11.86%) live domains use AWS (up from just 10 in 2011), 6 (0.64%) uses Azure, and 1 (0.11%) uses GitHub Pages.
  • In terms of mail providers, 105 (9.37%) use Office 365 and 33 (2.94%) use GSuite.
